Mahatma Phule Meusium

This was originally the residence of Mahatma Jyotiba Phule, the Maratha social activist who had been a strong proponent of women’s rights. This was transformed into a museum in 1890. It boasts of a diverse range of artefacts related to industry, agriculture, textile, handicrafts, geology, marble statues, stone carvings, arms, seals, armoury, etc. An array of stuffed animals is one of the highlights of this museum. This includes elephants, sea fish, etc. .

    In the 19th century, India witnessed a rise in various reformation activities in education, religion, culture and overall society. During this Renaissance period, several large museums were established in India. Mahatma Phule Museum was also laid down during this Renaissance period in 1890. Although back then, the Museum was known as Poona Industrial Museum, later it was renamed Lord Ray Museum.

    Needless to say, Mahatma Phule Museum is a great place to learn about the rich past and is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and culture.

How to reach the Mahatma Phule Meusium?

From Airport

The distance between the Pune International Airport and the Mahatma Phule Museum is only 10.5 kilometres.

Train, Bus –

The museum is in the Shivaji Nagar neighbourhood. Therefore, the Shivaji Nagar Railway Station, which is only 1.7 km from your destination, is the closest railway station.

Taxi Drive –

The museum is located in the city's commercial district. So, it has good road accessibility. To get to the museum, you can easily board a cab, bus, or rickshaw.
